# A/HRC/14/15 - Report of the Working Group on the Universal Periodic Review: Slovenia

**Document:** A/HRC/14/15  
**Date:** 18 March 2010  
**Session:** Human Rights Council, Fourteenth session  
**Agenda item 6:** Universal Periodic Review  

---

## I. Summary of the proceedings of the review process

### A. Presentation by the State under review

1. The delegation of Slovenia was headed by the State Secretary of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Mr. Andrej Logar. The delegation included representatives of the Ministry of Justice, Ministry of the Interior, Ministry of Labour, Family and Social Affairs, Ministry of Education and Sport, and the Government Office for Equal Opportunities.

2. Slovenia stated that it attaches great importance to the promotion and protection of human rights. Slovenia is party to eight core international human rights treaties and their optional protocols.

3. Slovenia highlighted its efforts to combat discrimination, including the adoption of the Implementation of the Principle of Equal Treatment Act (ZUNEO) in 2004.

4. Regarding the "erased" persons, Slovenia explained that the issue arose following independence in 1991 when citizens of other republics of the former Yugoslavia who did not apply for Slovenian citizenship were removed from the register of permanent residents.

## II. Conclusions and/or recommendations

**The following recommendations will be examined by Slovenia, which will provide responses in due time, but no later than the fourteenth session of the Human Rights Council:**

### 81. The recommendations formulated during the interactive dialogue and listed below enjoy the support of Slovenia:

81.1. Continue to make efforts towards the full realization of the rights of the child, particularly through raising awareness on non-violent methods of education and further promotion of the rights of children in accordance with international standards (Egypt);

81.2. Continue efforts to strengthen its national human rights institution, in line with the Paris Principles (Egypt);

81.3. Take further measures to address effectively the issue of discrimination against certain groups of the population, in particular the Roma community (Italy);

81.4. Continue with its efforts to ensure comprehensive legislation for the protection and promotion of human rights in accordance with international human rights instruments (Nigeria);

81.5. Continue efforts aimed at the promotion and protection of the rights of persons belonging to national minorities, in particular the Roma (Russian Federation);

81.6. Continue to strengthen efforts to achieve full enjoyment of human rights by Roma, including through effective implementation of the National Action Program for the Roma (Norway);

81.7. Take the necessary measures to ensure effective access of the Roma community to education, employment, health and housing (Bangladesh);

81.8. Develop and implement a comprehensive anti-discrimination strategy covering all grounds of discrimination prohibited under international human rights law (Netherlands);

81.9. Further intensify efforts to combat discrimination, in particular against the Roma community (Austria);

81.10. Continue its efforts to adopt comprehensive anti-discrimination legislation (Canada);

81.11. Speed up the process to grant compensation and restore permanent residence permits to the "erased" persons (Germany);

81.12. Take all measures necessary to provide remedy to the "erased" and find a lasting solution to this issue (Australia);

81.13. Consider strengthening efforts to combat racism and xenophobia (Philippines);

81.14. Take measures to prevent incitement to discrimination, including hate speech (Norway);

81.15. Intensify efforts to address racism, racial discrimination and xenophobia, including through awareness-raising campaigns and training of law enforcement officials (Brazil);

81.16. Strengthen measures to combat trafficking in human beings and provide appropriate protection and assistance to victims (Belarus);

81.17. Continue its efforts to combat trafficking in human beings (Russian Federation);

81.18. Continue efforts to raise awareness and combat stereotypes and prejudices against women (Brazil);

81.19. Take additional measures to ensure the effective participation of women in political and public life (Bangladesh);

81.20. Continue efforts to address the issue of violence against women, including domestic violence (Brazil);

81.21. Strengthen measures to prevent and combat violence against women, including domestic violence (Austria);

81.22. Take further measures to close the gender pay gap (Norway);

81.23. Continue efforts to ensure equal opportunities for women and men in the labour market (Netherlands);

81.24. Consider ratifying the Optional Protocol to the Convention against Torture (Czech Republic);

81.25. Expedite the ratification of the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities (Mexico);

81.26. Consider ratifying the International Convention on the Protection of the Rights of All Migrant Workers and Members of Their Families (Philippines);

81.27. Consider acceding to the International Convention on the Protection of the Rights of All Migrant Workers and Members of Their Families (Algeria);

81.28. Consider ratifying the International Convention for the Protection of All Persons from Enforced Disappearance (France);

---

### 82. The following recommendations will be examined by Slovenia, which will provide its response in due time:

82.1. Expedite the process of accession to the International Convention on the Protection of the Rights of All Migrant Workers and Members of Their Families (Egypt);

82.2. Ratify the International Convention on the Protection of the Rights of All Migrant Workers and Members of Their Families (Algeria);

82.3. Accede to the International Convention on the Protection of the Rights of All Migrant Workers and Members of Their Families (Mexico);

---

## III. Voluntary pledges and commitments

83. Slovenia made the following voluntary pledges and commitments:

(a) To continue its efforts to ensure full implementation of international human rights standards;

(b) To continue its cooperation with United Nations human rights mechanisms;

(c) To continue to work towards the elimination of all forms of discrimination.
