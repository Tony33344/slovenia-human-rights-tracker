# A/HRC/28/15 - Report of the Working Group on the Universal Periodic Review: Slovenia

**Document:** A/HRC/28/15  
**Date:** 10 December 2014  
**Session:** Human Rights Council, Twenty-eighth session  
**Agenda item 6:** Universal Periodic Review  

---

## I. Summary of the proceedings of the review process

### A. Presentation by the State under review

1. The delegation of Slovenia was headed by the State Secretary of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Mr. Bogdan Benko.

2. Slovenia stated that the promotion and protection of human rights remains a top priority. Since the first UPR cycle, Slovenia had ratified several international instruments, including the Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities and its Optional Protocol.

3. Slovenia presented its National Programme of Measures for Roma for 2010-2015, which focuses on improving the living conditions of Roma in areas such as education, employment, housing and health.

4. Regarding the "erased" persons, Slovenia informed that compensation proceedings were ongoing and that the Constitutional Court had issued important decisions on this matter.

---

## II. Conclusions and/or recommendations

118.2. Continue to strengthen human rights infrastructure and implementation of human rights obligations (South Africa);

118.3. Continue and further strengthen measures for the effective implementation of the legislation on combating discrimination (Austria);

118.4. Continue efforts to further strengthen the national human rights institution in line with the Paris Principles (Indonesia);

118.5. Continue to take effective measures to ensure that the Human Rights Ombudsman functions in full compliance with the Paris Principles (Russian Federation);

118.6. Continue efforts to ensure full compliance of the Human Rights Ombudsman with the Paris Principles and allocate adequate financial resources to this end (Mexico);

118.7. Continue implementing programmes to eliminate discrimination against the Roma community (Chile);

118.8. Strengthen efforts to combat discrimination against the Roma, in particular in the areas of employment, education, housing and health services (Austria);

118.9. Continue efforts to improve access of the Roma community to employment, education, health services and housing (Bangladesh);
